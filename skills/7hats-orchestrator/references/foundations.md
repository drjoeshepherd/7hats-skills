# Foundations

## Purpose
The 7 Hats model exists to help TPM/PO work in ambiguity without becoming a catch-all bottleneck.

## Core Principles
- Chaos is raw material; unmanaged chaos is risk.
- Name the mode before solving the problem.
- Switch hats intentionally, not by calendar pressure.
- Build mechanisms so uncertainty does not regenerate.
- Keep work item boundaries strict: Mission, Signal, Story are separate levels.

## Healthy Gray vs Toxic Gray
- Healthy gray:
  - Discovery uncertainty
  - Explicit trade-offs
  - Truth surfaced early
- Toxic gray:
  - Ownership avoidance
  - Hidden assumptions
  - Deferred decisions ("align later")

## TPM Boundary Discipline
- Own clarity, sequencing, and decision quality.
- Do not absorb hidden ownership from other functions.
- Convert ambiguity into explicit decisions, owners, and evidence.

## Hat Intent Snapshot
- Product: convert intent to measurable value.
- Researcher: replace opinions with evidence.
- Designer: turn empathy into robust flows.
- Engineer: translate outcomes into system constraints.
- Marketer: turn shipping into adoption.
- Entrepreneur: treat roadmap as a portfolio of bets.
- Meta: maintain the human operating conditions for all other hats.
